Priyo Adhikary's Research Interests

Overview

I am interested in the physics of strongly correlated electron systems, especially those that exhibit unconventional superconductivity. My research explores the superconducting properties of high-temperature cuprates e.g. YBCO, recently discovered over-doped Ba2CuO3.25, and infinite layer nickelates, as well as heavy fermions like CeCu2Si2 and CeCoIn5.

Key Contributions

  • Proposed a new mechanism of superconductivity in heavy fermions and mixed-valence systems.
  • My work on orbital dependence superconductivity in nickelates was validated by scanning tunnelling measurements.

Collaborative Work

During my PhD, I worked in close collaboration with various experimental groups, focusing on transport and superconducting properties, e.g., in materials like graphene and PdTe2.
